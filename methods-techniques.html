Methods & Techniques Overview

Geophysical methods we use

We are not afraid to take on any job, big or small, simple or complicated. We do it all and we do it well.

DCIP

DC Resistivity and Induced Polarization surveys for subsurface electrical properties mapping. Ideal for mineral exploration, groundwater detection, and environmental investigations.

Learn More

MRS

Magnetic Resonance Sounding (NMR) for direct water detection. Measures subsurface water content and permeability, perfect for groundwater resource evaluation and optimal well placement.

Learn More

GCM

Ground Conductivity Mapping for rapid reconnaissance surveys. Efficiently maps large areas to identify subsurface conductivity variations, archaeological features, and geological structures.

Learn More

TEM (Single Site)

Time-domain Electromagnetic sounding at fixed locations. Provides detailed vertical resistivity profiles for deep subsurface investigation, mineral exploration, and hydrogeological studies.

Learn More

TTEM

Helicopter-borne Time-domain Electromagnetic surveys. Covers large areas efficiently with high-resolution 3D subsurface mapping, ideal for regional mineral exploration and geological mapping.

Learn More

Reflection Seismics

High-resolution seismic reflection surveys for detailed subsurface imaging. Maps geological layers, faults, and structures with exceptional precision for oil and gas exploration and geological research.

Learn More

Refraction Seismics

Seismic refraction surveys for shallow subsurface characterization. Determines bedrock depth, soil layer thickness, and seismic velocities for geotechnical and engineering applications.

Learn More

Gravimetrics

Gravity surveys measuring density contrasts in the subsurface. Detects voids, caves, buried valleys, and density variations for geological mapping and resource exploration.

Learn More

Geomagnetics

Magnetic surveys detecting variations in Earth's magnetic field. Maps magnetic anomalies for mineral exploration, archaeological investigations, and detecting buried metallic objects.

Learn More

Borehole Logging

In-situ borehole measurements providing detailed subsurface information. Records physical properties directly within boreholes for geological characterization and resource evaluation.
